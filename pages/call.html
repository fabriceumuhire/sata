SMART AFRICA TRUST ALLIANCE - SATA Call

Background

The concept of SATA builds on existing frameworks of digital identity across the African continent, namely the “Principles on Identification for Sustainable Development: Toward the Digital Age”, the International Telecommunications Union (ITU) “Digital Identity for Development” initiative, and the “African Trust Framework for Interoperability” developed by the United Nations Economic Commission for Africa (UNECA).

Building on the Smart Africa Manifesto and its vision to transform Africa into a Single Digital Market, the Smart Africa Alliance has adopted a Blueprint on the interoperability of digital identity systems during its 9th Board Meeting on the 7th of December 2020.

The Blueprint, championed by the Republic of Benin and co-drafted with many international partners of the Alliance, laid out the concept of the “Smart Africa Trust Alliance” (SATA).

The development of SATA will be closely aligned with the implementation of the African Union Digital Transformation Strategy and the African Union Interoperability Framework for Digital ID.

Objectives

The vision behind SATA is to include and empower everyone to be part of the transformation of Africa into a Single Digital Market. SATA is a trust mechanism for data exchanges in Africa. SATA enables persons, both natural and legal, to prove their identities safely and securely across multiple situations via robust digital mechanisms, generating new possibilities for reliable and trusted cross-border relationships.

By creating a framework that enables the trusted and controlled movement of people, data, money, services and goods, SATA stimulates the growth of intra-African trade, directly supporting the African Continental Free Trade Area (AfCFTA).

The Founding Features of the Smart Africa Trust Alliance

The Smart Africa Trust Alliance (SATA) brings together State and non-State actors across Africa to form an innovative public-private partnership. SATA is a self-governing, supranational independent institution, with voluntary membership. Member States remain at the heart of the SATA governance.

At its core sit the SATA Members supported by the Smart Africa Secretariat. A federated governance model builds trust amongst all parties involved, whilst also ensuring that the sovereignty of the Member States remains respected.

A supervisory function is fulfilled by SATA Members, agreeing upon a mutually acceptable framework of sustainable principles, procedures and standards that underpins SATA and its development of a rigorous and respected certification process. The platform is based on these principles and requirements. It is built on open-source and open-standards technologies uniquely assembled for this purpose.

Beginning with the Smart Africa Member States and their respective regulators, SATA is an expanding continental consortium of digital ID and service providers who exchange information with each other following a set of mutually agreed guidelines and supported by a certification and audit system. These are a universal guarantee of trust and quality demonstrating that, because an entity has passed the SATA certification process, electronic transactions may take place efficiently and legally, thereby assuring that user data is kept private and handled safely.

To avoid a one-size-fits-all approach, SATA designs flexible and context-aware solutions to support the maximal inclusion of hitherto marginalized populations. Support for multiple Levels of Assurance allows for the effective management of risks, always ensuring the integrity of interactions in different situations and allowing Members to tailor services to users’ specific needs.
