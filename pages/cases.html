SMART AFRICA TRUST ALLIANCE - USE CASES

Use Cases

The Smart Africa Trust Alliance (SATA) is a continental network of digital ID and service providers who will exchange ID information with each other in order to facilitate cross border services following a set of mutually agreed guidelines and supported by a certification.

SATA Pilot Projects

One Time SIM Registration

Cross-border movements in Africa remain largely informal because of limited access to trusted IDs,preventing access to SIM card in the visited country.

Impact of One Time SIM Registration:

  • Reduce the social and financial exclusion of travelling citizens
  • Help governments expand communication campaigns including life-saving security information.
  • Create new markets and simplify registration process for Mobile Network Operators certified by SATAs.
  • Support the formalization of cross-border economic activities, reduction of fraud and the increase of tax revenues.

One Time Business Registration

Business registration is necessary for companies who want to establish local branches, bank accounts for conducting business in another African country.

Impact of One Time Business Registration:

  • Reduce costs associated with business registration in Smart Africa Member States
  • Improve the efficiency and effectiveness of border compliances
  • Create linkages with Customer Due Diligence systems to simplify access to financial services.
